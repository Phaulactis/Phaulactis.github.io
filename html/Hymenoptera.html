Hymenoptera

Information Key

Description

Hymenoptera is a large and diverse order of insects, comprising more than 150,000 described species worldwide. This group includes ants, bees, wasps, and sawflies. Hymenopterans are characterized by their two pairs of wings, with the front wings larger than the hind wings, and a narrow waist that separates the thorax from the abdomen.

Ecology

Many species of Hymenoptera are important pollinators, playing a vital role in the reproductive success of many plant species. Others are predators or parasitoids of other insects, and are therefore important natural enemies of pests in agricultural and horticultural settings. Some species, such as ants and social wasps, have complex social structures, with individuals working together to care for their young and protect their colonies.

Distribution

Hymenopterans are found on all continents except antarctica in a wide range of habitats.
