Ichneumonidae

Information Key

Description

Ichneumonids superficially resemble other wasps, though they usually have more antennal segments and female ichneumonids have an unmodified ovipositor which they use to lay eggs inside or on their host. Ichneumonids inject venom with the egg, but only larger species use the ovipositor in defense. Males do not possess stingers or ovipositors. Ichneumonids are different from their sister group, Braconidae, mainly based on wing venation and metasoma structure. Most ichneumonids have a flexible suture between tergites 2 and 3, while these tergites are fused in braconids.

Ecology

All ichneumonids are parasitoids, maturing inside other insects or arthropods. Some species are even hyperparasitoids, meaning their hosts are other parasitoids. This behavior makes them a key natural pest control. The adult wasps tend to have a more peaceful lifestyle by being important pollinators.

Distribution

Ichneumonids are found on all continents except antarctica. They have traditionally been seen as a rather rare group in the tropics with their main diversity being found in temperate regions, but a wave of recently described species from tropical regions is now challenging this belief.
