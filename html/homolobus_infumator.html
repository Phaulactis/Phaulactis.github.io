Homolobus infumator

Description

A distinctive species, easily recognized by the dark coloration of the head, thorax, mesonotum and apical part of hind tibia. most of the legs and part of mesosternum and propodeum orange to yellowish brown. marginal cell of hind wing with cross vein. Malar space is around 0.6 times the basal width of the mandible. Wing vein 1A + 2A straight and tarsal claws with a small and sharp subapical tooth.

Ecology

A thelytokous species with two generations each year. All known hosts are Geometrid moths.

Distribution

Known from much of the palaearctic. Distribution in Sweden is porly understood but it is known from southern and central parts of the country.
